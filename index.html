With Gratitude

Thank You

"

To you — yes, you, reading this right now — thank you for being here. Genuinely.

I'll be honest: I could not have drawn this version of my life on a map. A soon-to-be Doctor... of Pharmacy. God's plans were originally not mine — but through patience, a whole lot of listening, and some very persistent nudging from the people closest to me, this is now a soon-to-be reality.

There are so many people I owe gratitude to, but let me start at the top. Jesus. Yes — I know, I know — cliché incoming: "I wouldn't be here without Him." But it's just simply true. My faith and my relationship with Him is ever-changing, ever-evolving, and with Him as my center and my number one, life has purpose. I have purpose — to be a light, just as He was, is, and always will be.

Next is my wife, Diamond. She refused to let me settle. She pushed me to believe in myself and stood right beside me as my life partner through more challenges than either of us expected. She beautifully remains my greatest support system and the love of my life. This one's for you too, babe.

And then there are my parents. My dad, Clifford Jr. — whose consistent demand (ahem, "recommendation") to go meet his local pharmacist quite literally started this entire journey. What began as a conversation became a career as a pharmacy technician in training, and now — a pharmacist. I see what you did there, Dad.

And my mother — Patricia. Beautiful, courageous, forward-thinking Patricia. She called me Dr. Jackson from the moment I was accepted into pharmacy school — not once wavering in that belief. Though she fought hard to be here in person, she is now with the Lord. Mom, thank you for being my rock, my gentle friend, my biggest champion, my mama bear. I love you. I miss you. And I know you're watching.

My journey doesn't end here — not even close. But we can't move forward without making a proper Jackson-Lindsey moment out of this milestone. And as I get older, I understand exactly why. I am a grateful and very willing participant.

With all my love — thank you for celebrating with me.

* No, this was not written by AI — despite the suspicious volume of em dashes. These are my real words. I just clean up nice.

— CJ3

Clifford Jackson III, Pharm.D. · Texas Southern University · Class of 2026
